Personal Statement

My intended career in robotics is rooted in the belief that robotics should serve humans. My goal as an engineer is to design systems capable of handling hazardous and repetitive tasks, freeing people to focus on more unique challenges. To achieve this goal, society must move from automation to collaboration. This requires robots that are mechanically reliable and intuitive enough to operate safely alongside humans.

I first explored robotics through mechanical engineering, designing an over-terrain vehicle (OTV) drive train and navigation algorithm. This navigation system utilized an ultrasound sensor for obstacle detection, relaying the distance data to a WiFi module to calculate the necessary path corrections within a three-degree rotation tolerance. Watching the OTV successfully navigate the obstacle course, avoiding obstructions and autonomously pathfinding to the end site, further reinforced my interest in robotics. This project was one of my first lessons in the necessity of hardware and software synergy.

Later, while learning to code through game development, I eventually experimented with computer vision to create an automated target-acquisition system using OpenCV. This project required me to build a system that could dynamically detect visual patterns, predict spatial position, and translate the data into mechanical inputs. At the time, I did not fully realize I was exploring the fundamentals of machine perception; I was simply fascinated by how a program could interpret and react to its surroundings. This experience of the interaction between visual data and physical action is what drew me to AI and further towards robotics.

These projects, one focused on physical navigation and the other on digital perception, have shaped my core belief: robotics requires deep interdisciplinary respect. My work with the OTV taught me that a programmer cannot ignore the constraints of physics, while my experience with OpenCV showed me that an engineer cannot overlook the intelligence embedded in the machine.

Moving forward, my goal is to develop autonomous systems that function as a partnership between humans and robots. I intend to focus my career on creating adaptive interfaces where high-level machine perception informs low-level mechanical control, allowing robots to navigate the nuanced unpredictability of practical environments. By integrating computerized intelligence and physical reliability, I hope to build systems that empower the people they serve.
